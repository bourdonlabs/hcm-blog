There's a moment that happens to almost everyone in Ho Chi Minh City. You've been down in the heat and the traffic and the beautiful, relentless noise of street level all day, and then you take an elevator up forty-something floors, step out onto a rooftop, and the entire city opens up below you like a map made of lights.

It's spectacular. Every time.

Saigon's rooftop bar scene has matured enormously over the past decade. What was once a handful of hotel bars with great views and mediocre drinks has become a genuine cocktail culture — mixologists who know what they're doing, thoughtful menus, and enough variety to suit every mood from pre-dinner sundowners to full-send Saturday night.

Here are the eight best, ranked with brutal honesty.

## The 8 Best Rooftop Bars in Ho Chi Minh City

**1. Chill Sky Bar — AB Tower, 76A Le Lai, District 1**

The one that everyone knows and nobody can argue with. Chill Sky Bar sits on the 26th floor of AB Tower with an unobstructed 360-degree view of District 1 and beyond. The bar itself is gorgeous — curved white structure, infinity-edge pool that catches the sunset beautifully, and an atmosphere that somehow manages to be both glamorous and relaxed.

Cocktail recommendation: their signature "Saigon Mule" (lemongrass-infused vodka, ginger beer, fresh lime) is the unofficial drink of the city's rooftop scene. Dress code is smart casual — no flip flops, no tank tops. Prices: 180,000–350,000 VND for cocktails.

**2. EON Heli Bar — Bitexco Financial Tower, 2 Hai Trieu, District 1**

On the 52nd floor of the Bitexco Tower — Saigon's iconic helipad skyscraper — EON Heli Bar offers the highest rooftop bar experience in the city center. The views are extraordinary: you can see across the entire city on a clear day, the Saigon River curving through it in both directions.

The cocktail menu is creative and changes seasonally. Go for the city skyline cocktail flight if it's on the menu. Book ahead on weekends because this one fills up. Prices: 200,000–400,000 VND.

**3. Shri Restaurant & Lounge — Centec Tower, 72-74 Nguyen Thi Minh Khai, District 3**

Less known than the big D1 names, Shri is beloved by locals who know the city's best-kept rooftop secrets. The 23rd floor space has an elegant, more intimate feel — proper lounge furniture, soft lighting, an open kitchen doing serious food alongside excellent drinks.

The wine list is one of the best at any rooftop venue in the city. The view down Nguyen Thi Minh Khai at night — all motorbike lights and illuminated trees — is genuinely cinematic. Prices: 150,000–320,000 VND.

**4. Social Club Rooftop — Hotel des Arts Saigon, 76-78 Nguyen Thi Minh Khai, District 3**

The most photogenic rooftop in the city, full stop. The Social Club Rooftop at Hotel des Arts is where Saigon's creative and fashion crowd comes to be seen, and with views like this, you understand why. The pool terrace glows at golden hour in a way that looks edited even in real life.

The bar program is run with genuine care — the bartenders know their stuff and the seasonal cocktail menu always has something interesting. Book a table and order the bar snacks; the truffle fries alone are worth the visit. Prices: 180,000–380,000 VND.

**5. Bar Betta — District 1**

The new-school entry on this list. Bar Betta is a more intimate rooftop cocktail bar that's been making noise among Saigon's cocktail cognoscenti for its serious approach to craft drinks. The menu reads more like a restaurant — provenance of spirits matters here, techniques are explained, the bartenders are enthusiastic without being pretentious.

The view is good rather than spectacular, but the drinks are some of the best on any rooftop in the city. Go if you care about what's in your glass. Prices: 180,000–350,000 VND.

**6. Saigon Saigon Rooftop Bar — Caravelle Hotel, 19 Lam Son Square, District 1**

The historic one. Saigon Saigon Bar on the 9th floor of the legendary Caravelle Hotel has been part of the city's story since 1959 — war correspondents drank here, diplomats drank here, and now you drink here with a view of the Opera House and the lit-up square below. It's less "spectacular city views" and more "deeply atmospheric Saigon history."

The cocktails are solid, the prices are reasonable for a hotel bar, and the feeling of drinking where so much history happened is something you can't find 40 floors up in a glass tower. Prices: 150,000–280,000 VND.

**7. Le Belhamy Rooftop — 50 Pham Hong Thai, District 1**

A relative newcomer that's quickly become a local favorite for its relaxed vibe and good-value drinks. Le Belhamy has a more casual, lived-in feel than the polished hotel bars — mismatched furniture, plants everywhere, a genuinely friendly crowd. The view catches the Saigon River on one side and the D1 skyline on the other.

Come here for a relaxed evening rather than a special occasion, and bring cash — they often have card machine issues. Prices: 100,000–250,000 VND.

**8. The Summit Rooftop — Novotel Saigon Centre, 167 Hai Ba Trung, District 3**

The reliable all-rounder. Summit is perfect for those who want consistent quality — good cocktails, reliable service, solid views, no surprises. It's popular with the hotel's business traveler crowd and works well for a civilized pre-dinner drink with someone you've just met or need to impress.

The happy hour (usually 5–7pm) offers solid discounts and makes it one of the better value propositions in the city. Prices: 150,000–300,000 VND.

## When to Go and What to Know

**Golden hour** (roughly 5:30–6:30pm from October to March, 5–6pm in summer) is when rooftop bars are at their absolute best. The city turns amber and gold, the heat breaks slightly, and the first cold drink of the evening hits perfectly.

Most places have a smart casual dress code — clean shorts and a collared shirt will work almost everywhere. The big hotel bars enforce it more strictly than the boutique spots.

**Book ahead on weekends.** Chill Sky Bar and EON in particular fill up by 6pm on Friday and Saturday. Walk-ins are hit and miss. A 15-minute advance booking is almost always worth it.

And if someone tells you the views aren't worth the prices — they're wrong. In a city this beautiful, altitude has real value.
